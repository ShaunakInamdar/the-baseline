# Only Plans — Onboarding Script (ADHD Layer)

> Extended onboarding for users with ADHD or focus struggles.
> Builds on top of the base script with energy mapping, avoidance patterns, and motivation style.

---

> **Research note:** The additions in this script are grounded in ADHD coaching and CBT literature.
> Three areas are most impactful beyond the base layer:
> (1) **Energy mapping** — ADHD brains have non-linear energy cycles, so knowing peak hours is critical for scheduling calls effectively.
> (2) **The emotional "why"** — ADHD is interest-driven, not importance-driven, meaning a task needs personal meaning to stick.
> (3) **Failure recovery preferences** — how the agent handles a missed call or unfinished task is just as important as the task itself.

---

```
AGENT: Hey, welcome to Only Plans. I'm your accountability partner.
       Before we get started, I want to get to know you a little —
       so I can actually be useful, not just another reminder you ignore.
       This'll take about 5 minutes. Ready?

--- SECTION 1: ENERGY & TIME ---

AGENT: First — when during the day do you feel most like yourself?
       Like, when's your brain actually online?

       [morning / afternoon / evening / it varies / I have no idea]

AGENT: Got it. And are there times of day that are completely off-limits for me?
       Think: gym, commute, kids, meals, sleep.

       [user responds freely]

AGENT: Perfect — I'll stay out of those windows.

--- SECTION 2: YOUR GOAL ---

AGENT: Now — what's the one thing you most want to get done?
       Don't overthink it. What keeps coming back to your mind?

       [user responds]

AGENT: Why does that matter to you? Like, what changes when it's done?

       [user responds — this anchors emotional motivation]

AGENT: Okay. What's the smallest first step you could take on that today —
       something you could do in the next hour if you had to?

       [user responds — AI creates first task card from this]

--- SECTION 3: PATTERNS ---

AGENT: Quick one — what do you tend to avoid the most?
       Not judging, I just want to know what I'm working with.

       [user responds]

AGENT: And what usually gets in the way — is it getting started,
       staying focused, or finishing things off?

       [getting started / staying focused / finishing / all three / depends]

--- SECTION 4: HOW YOU WORK BEST ---

AGENT: Have you tried productivity apps or systems before?
       What actually worked, even a little?

       [user responds — helps agent mirror what already resonates]

AGENT: Do you work better with more structure — like a clear schedule —
       or do you need some flexibility built in?

       [structure / flexibility / mix of both]

--- SECTION 5: ACCOUNTABILITY STYLE ---

AGENT: How do you want me to treat you when things go sideways?

       [tough love — be straight with me
        gentle — be kind, I'm trying my best
        just the facts — no emotion, just what's next]

AGENT: And what motivates you more — earning a reward when you finish,
       or the discomfort of NOT finishing?

       [rewards / consequences / both / neither, just remind me]

AGENT: Last thing — how often should I check in?

       [a few times a day / once a day / a few times a week]

AGENT: And if you don't pick up when I call — what should I do?

       [try again in 30 min / send a text / leave it, I'll call back / keep trying]

--- SECTION 6: CONFIRM & CREATE ---

AGENT: Okay. Here's what I've got:

       Your first task is: [TASK]
       Your first step is: [STEP]
       I'll call you at [TIME], and if you don't pick up I'll [FALLBACK].
       I'll keep it [TONE] and focus on [MOTIVATION STYLE].

       Sound right?

       [yes / change something]

AGENT: Perfect. I'll create your first task card now.
       Talk soon.
```
